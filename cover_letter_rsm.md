Dear Editor,

Please consider the manuscript "Outcome Reporting Bias Risk in 403 Cochrane Meta-Analyses: An Excess Significance Approach" for publication in Research Synthesis Methods.

Outcome reporting bias remains difficult to detect at scale because direct assessment usually requires protocol-to-publication comparison. This study applies the Ioannidis-Trikalinos excess significance test together with heterogeneity, outlier, and precision asymmetry indicators to 403 Cochrane meta-analyses from the Pairwise70 dataset. The resulting composite framework identifies 16.1% of reviews as high risk and shows a clear gradient in heterogeneity and outlier burden across risk classes.

The manuscript fits the journal because it offers a practical meta-research method for screening selective reporting risk across large review collections. The analysis is fully reproducible, uses a large curated dataset, and is designed to complement rather than replace established frameworks such as ORBIT and PRISMA 2020 reporting expectations.

The manuscript has not been published or submitted elsewhere. The author has approved the submission. Analysis code is available in the linked repository, and the Pairwise70 source dataset is currently available from its public GitHub repository while archive DOI registration remains pending.
